CHERRY TREE | flowering cherry tree

A cherry tree is a deciduous tree belonging to the genus Prunus, which is native to regions of Europe, Asia, and North America. Cherry trees are cultivated for their edible fruit, which is commonly used in jams, jellies, pies, and other desserts. There are many different species of cherry trees, each with its own unique characteristics, but most are small to medium-sized trees that produce showy flowers in the spring and colorful fruit in the summer.
